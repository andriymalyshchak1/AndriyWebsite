How I think about product intuition→

Most product decisions aren't made in meetings — they're made in the quiet moments when you notice something feels off. This is about building the habit of paying attention to that feeling and learning to trust it without losing rigor. It's less about frameworks and more about the daily practice of staying honest with yourself.

What am I actually optimizing for→

Two months of major transitions — graduation, a volcano in Guatemala at 2am, moving to San Francisco — forced the question: what is actually worth optimizing for? This is about the pull of San Francisco's single monoculture, the risk of rationalizing your own decisions, and why stepping away sharpens the habit of catching yourself before you build airtight reasoning around a choice you've already made.
